First Aid Guide

Step-by-Step Instructions for Medical Emergencies

Learn how to handle common emergencies with our easy-to-follow guides, complete with illustrations and videos to ensure you’re prepared when it matters most.

First Aid for Common Emergencies

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

  1. Check for responsiveness: Shake the person and shout, "Are you okay?"
  2. Call for help: Dial emergency services immediately.
  3. Start chest compressions: Place hands on the center of the chest and push hard and fast (100-120 compressions per minute).
  4. Give rescue breaths: After 30 compressions, give 2 breaths (if trained).
  5. Continue until help arrives or the person responds.

Choking

  1. Ask if they can speak or cough: If not, they’re choking.
  2. Perform the Heimlich maneuver: Stand behind, wrap arms around their waist, and thrust upward into the abdomen.
  3. Repeat up to 5 times, checking if the blockage is cleared.
  4. If the person becomes unresponsive, start CPR.
  5. Call emergency services if the blockage persists.

Burns

  1. Remove from the heat source: Ensure safety first.
  2. Cool the burn: Run cool (not cold) water over the burn for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove tight items: Take off jewelry or clothing near the burn area.
  4. Cover with a sterile dressing: Use a clean, non-stick bandage.
  5. Seek medical help for severe burns (larger than 3 inches or on the face/hands).

Severe Bleeding

  1. Apply direct pressure: Use a clean cloth or your hand to press on the wound.
  2. Elevate the injury: Raise the injured area above the heart if possible.
  3. Use a tourniquet if necessary: Apply above the wound for severe bleeding.
  4. Do not remove the cloth: Add more layers if blood soaks through.
  5. Get emergency help: Call for professional assistance immediately.

Fractures

  1. Do not move the injured area: Keep it as still as possible.
  2. Immobilize the injury: Use a splint or sling to support the limb.
  3. Apply ice: Use a cold pack wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling.
  4. Elevate if possible: Raise the limb to reduce swelling.
  5. Seek medical help: Fractures require professional evaluation.
