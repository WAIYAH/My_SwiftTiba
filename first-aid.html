First Aid Guide

Step-by-step emergency instructions that could save a life. Follow these guides carefully and always call emergency services for serious situations.

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

Critical â€” Act Immediately
  1. Check responsiveness. Tap the person's shoulder and shout, "Are you okay?" Look for normal breathing for no more than 10 seconds.
  2. Call for help. Call 999 or 112 immediately or ask someone nearby to call. Put the phone on speaker if you're alone.
  3. Begin chest compressions. Place the heel of your hand on the center of the chest. Push hard and fast at 100-120 compressions per minute, pressing down at least 2 inches deep.
  4. Give rescue breaths. Tilt the head back, lift the chin, pinch the nose shut, and give 2 rescue breaths. Follow a cycle of 30 compressions to 2 breaths.
  5. Continue until help arrives. Do not stop CPR until emergency services arrive, an AED is available, or the person starts breathing normally.

Choking (Heimlich Maneuver)

Critical â€” Act Immediately
  1. Assess the situation. Check if the person can speak, cough, or breathe. If they can cough forcefully, encourage them to keep coughing.
  2. Perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver). Stand behind the person, wrap your arms around their waist, place your fist above the navel, and give quick upward thrusts.
  3. Repeat up to 5 times. Alternate between 5 back blows (between shoulder blades) and 5 abdominal thrusts until the object is dislodged.
  4. If the person becomes unresponsive, lower them to the ground and begin CPR. Check the mouth for visible obstructions before giving rescue breaths.
  5. Call emergency services (999/112) if the object does not come out or the person loses consciousness at any point.

Severe Bleeding

Critical â€” Act Immediately
  1. Apply direct pressure. Use a clean cloth, bandage, or clothing to press firmly on the wound. Maintain constant, steady pressure.
  2. Elevate the wound. If possible, raise the injured area above the level of the heart to help slow the bleeding.
  3. Use a tourniquet if necessary. For severe limb bleeding that won't stop, apply a tourniquet 2-3 inches above the wound. Note the time it was applied.
  4. Don't remove soaked cloth. If the first cloth soaks through, add more layers on top instead of removing the original. Removing may disrupt clotting.
  5. Call emergency services. Call 999/112 immediately for severe or uncontrolled bleeding. Keep the person warm and still until help arrives.

Burns

Urgent â€” Seek Care
  1. Remove from the heat source. Ensure the person is no longer in contact with the source of the burn. Remove any smoldering clothing carefully.
  2. Cool with running water. Hold the burned area under cool (not cold or ice) running water for 10-15 minutes. This is the most important first aid step.
  3. Remove tight items. Gently remove jewelry, belts, or tight clothing near the burned area before swelling begins.
  4. Cover with a sterile dressing. Apply a loose, non-stick sterile bandage. Do not apply butter, toothpaste, or other home remedies to the burn.
  5. Seek medical help for severe burns (larger than 3 inches), burns on the face, hands, feet, genitals, or over joints, and all chemical or electrical burns.

Fractures (Broken Bones)

Urgent â€” Seek Care
  1. Don't move the injured area. Keep the person still. Any movement can cause further damage, especially with spine or neck injuries.
  2. Immobilize with a splint or sling. Use a rigid object (board, rolled newspaper) to stabilize the area above and below the fracture. Pad with soft material.
  3. Apply ice. Place a cold pack wrapped in cloth on the injured area for 15-20 minutes to reduce swelling. Do not apply ice directly to skin.
  4. Elevate the injured area. If possible, raise the limb above heart level to help reduce swelling and pain.
  5. Seek medical help immediately. All suspected fractures need professional medical evaluation. Call 999/112 if the fracture is severe, open, or involves the spine.
